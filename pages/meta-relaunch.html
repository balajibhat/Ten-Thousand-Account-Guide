Meta Ads — Relaunch Strategy

Meta was Ten Thousand's primary paid social growth channel through 2025. The account went offline in January 2026 due to a payment issue. This page captures what we know from 2025 performance, the strategic approach for bringing Meta back, and how to think about the channel post-relaunch.

Expect a 2-week re-learning phase. Meta's algorithm needs time to recalibrate after a multi-month shutdown. Frame the first two weeks as experimental in all client communications. Do not judge performance during this window by the same standards as steady-state campaigns.

Andromeda Context & Data Reliability

Meta's Andromeda algorithm update shifted the balance from audience tuning to creative quality. This has direct implications for how we interpret historical data and plan going forward.

Pre-Andromeda data is unreliable. The previous agency's testing methodology (Jun-Sep 2025) was built around audience segmentation that Andromeda has made obsolete. Creative was not the variable being tested — audiences were. That data does not predict post-Andromeda performance. When pulling historical creative winners, focus on the Oct 2025 - Jan 2026 window only. Scrub any BFCM period data (late Nov - early Dec) as it reflects promotional behavior, not organic demand.
What Andromeda means for us Creative quality is now the primary lever for Meta performance. The algorithm handles audience discovery and optimization — our job is to feed it strong creative, not micro-segment audiences. This is why the relaunch structure is simplified to 4 campaigns with broad targeting, not the 100+ campaigns the previous agency ran.

Color & Print Creative Rules

Allowed

A creator wearing a discontinued color is fine as long as the color is not the focus of the ad. If the ad is about the product, the fit, or the lifestyle — and the color just happens to be discontinued — it can run.

Not Allowed

Never run ads that call out unavailable colors. If the ad copy, overlay, or focal point highlights a specific colorway that is no longer in stock, it cannot run. This creates a broken purchase path.

Discontinued prints must never appear in ads. Unlike solid colors that blend into the background, prints are distinctive and recognizable. A viewer who sees a print in an ad will look for that specific print on the site.

Conversion Lag & Reporting Cadence

Meta conversion attribution lags significantly. Approximately 30% more conversions appear 2-5 days after initial reporting. Any performance read taken within the first 48 hours of a reporting period is incomplete. Data needs roughly 1 week to fully settle. This means weekend-over-weekend comparisons always look worse unless the lag is accounted for — the most recent weekend's data is always undercounted relative to the prior one.
Practical implication When reporting Meta performance to the client, always note how many days of settled data the report reflects. A "bad week" may just be an "incomplete week." Internal optimization decisions on Meta should use data that is at least 5 days old for a clean read.

Lessons from 2025

What Worked — Bring Back

Consolidated ASC campaigns. The top two campaigns by spend were both Advantage+ Shopping focused on Interval Shorts. Massive volume, stable performance.

DPA/DABA catalog carousels. Top two ads by ROAS were dynamic catalog carousels at 20.64x and 17.39x. Showing users what they browsed or what the algorithm predicts they want is the single most efficient format.

Retention/Reactivation. Past-purchaser targeting hit 5.37x ROAS on $83K spend. With a multi-month Meta gap, there is a backlog of lapsed customers to reactivate.

Product pack static images. "Interval Pack" drove 107 purchases at 1.21x ROAS. Packs increase AOV.

Athlete testimonials (UGC). Grant McCartney testimonial and "3FEELING" video were top prospecting spenders. Authentic creator content works for acquisition.

Test-then-scale framework. The [c2-test] / [c2-scale] naming convention allowed structured testing before budget allocation.

What Failed — Do Not Repeat

Over-segmented funnel campaigns. Upper/Mid/Lower acquisition splits were wildly inefficient. Upper_Acquisition spent $31K for 5 purchases (0.02x ROAS). Mid_Acquisition spent $28K for 36 purchases (0.15x ROAS). Meta's algorithm works best with liquidity, not micro-segmentation.

Reach/Awareness objectives. Campaigns optimized for Reach or Engagement consistently failed to drive revenue. ROAS below 0.20x. Do not run any campaign that is not optimized for the Purchase event.

Product concentration risk. 16 of top 20 ads promoted shorts. Tops and pants had almost no dedicated budget. Missed Q4/Q1 revenue on non-shorts products.

Generic lifestyle imagery. Ads without clear product focus or bundle offer underperformed direct product-focused creatives.

Recommended Campaign Structure

Simplified from 100 historical campaigns to 4 core campaigns. Every campaign optimizes for Purchase. No funnel segmentation. No Reach/Awareness objectives.

Campaign 1: ASC — Broad Prospecting Primary scale lever
Objective Sales (Purchase). Advantage+ Shopping Campaign format. Let Meta's algorithm find buyers with maximum liquidity.
Creative Best-performing historical creatives: Interval Shorts product shots, pack bundles, athlete testimonials (Bryce, Grant McCartney). New UGC pipeline. Mix of static + video.
Audience Broad. ASC handles targeting. Set existing customer cap at 10-20% to bias toward new acquisition.
Budget approach Start conservative. Ramp 15-20% every 3-4 days if CPA is at or below target. Do not front-load spend during learning phase.
Campaign 2: DABA — Catalog Prospecting High-efficiency format
Objective Sales (Purchase). Dynamic Ads for Broad Audiences. Uses the product catalog to find new customers based on intent signals.
Creative Dynamic carousel format. The 2025 DPA carousels were the single best-performing ad format (20x+ ROAS). Requires a current, clean product catalog with accurate inventory, pricing, and imagery.
Key requirement Product catalog must be current. OOS products suppressed. The supplemental feed built for Google Shopping should also cover Meta's catalog. Coordinate with Patrick on inventory.
Campaign 3: DPA — Retargeting Proven efficient
Objective Sales (Purchase). Dynamic Product Ads targeting website visitors, cart abandoners, and engaged users. Historically the most efficient campaign type.
Audiences Website visitors (7d, 14d, 30d). Add-to-cart (7d). View content (7d, 14d). As Meta activity resumes, layer in video viewers and post engagers as those pools build.
Ramp-up consideration Retargeting pools will be thin after a multi-month offline period. This campaign will ramp slowly by nature. Don't force budget into it early. Let the ASC and DABA campaigns fill the pools first.
Campaign 4: Retention / Reactivation High ROAS opportunity
Objective Sales (Purchase). Target past purchasers who haven't bought in 60-180 days. After months of Meta silence, there is a backlog of dormant customers who haven't seen a Meta ad from TT.
Creative approach New product drops, new colorways, seasonal messaging. Align Meta retention creative with the email calendar product moments (same products, different channel).
Historical signal 2025 retention campaign hit 5.37x ROAS on $83K spend. This is the highest-efficiency campaign type. With a multi-month gap, the reactivation opportunity is even larger than normal.

Channel Coordination Principles

Snap policy when Meta is live: Do NOT pause Snap. Reduce to a "low and defensible" level. If Snap CPA stays at $25-$40, keep it running. The goal is to observe how attributed performance looks when both channels are on simultaneously. Once Meta reaches consistent scale, reassess Snap's role and budget share.
Reporting approach With multiple paid social channels running, report a weekly table showing percentage of total spend by channel and percentage of attributed conversions by channel. This gives the client a clear view of where pCPA pressure is coming from and makes it easy to identify which channel to pull back if blended pCPA rises above target.
X/Twitter: In negotiation (pushing for a $50K spend commitment + $25K credit, not the $200K Meta proposed). Start X post-Meta-relaunch once Meta is stable. Patrick's preference: prove the channel before committing large spend, same approach as Thursday Boots.

How to Measure Meta Performance

Meta KPI Framework — Two Phases
Metric Learning Phase (first 2 weeks) Steady State
Meta Purchase ROAS Tracking only. Do not react to daily swings. Look at 7-day rolling average. Target: 1.5x+ (2025 blended average). Stretch: 1.77x (April 2025 level).
Meta CPP Expect $100-130 during learning (in line with 2025 January ramp). Target: sub-$90. Stretch: sub-$80 (April 2025 level).
Blended pCPA Will rise when Meta spend comes online. Have the Evergreen-only pCPA ready for client. Frame Meta as experimental during ramp. Target: ≤$50. Client's north star guardrail. If Meta is making this impossible, reduce Meta budget.
New Customer V-ROAS New metric from Patrick. Floor: 2.9x. Break-even range: 2.5-2.9x. Track from day one even during learning. Must be above 2.9x consistently. This is the metric Patrick is building the forecast around.
Retargeting Pool Size Watch it grow. 75%+ video viewers, post engagers, website visitors. Thin at launch. Should build steadily. Healthy retargeting pools = lower DPA/retargeting CPA. This is the leading indicator.
Client communication during ramp: Do not alarm on learning-phase metrics. Frame as: "We're in the ramp window. Here's what we're seeing. Here's what's expected. We'll have clean reads in two weeks." Always pair blended pCPA with the Evergreen-only number as the safety blanket.
